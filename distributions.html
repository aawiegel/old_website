Demonstrating Sampling Distributions

This shiny app demonstrates three common probability distributions and the effect of sampling from those distributions.

Distributions Modeled

Ther user can change the size of the population, sample size, and features of the distribution (e.g., the mean). This allows the user to get a sense for whether the central limit theorem will apply to their sample given estimates of the population distribution.

Distributions modeled and their features:

  • Normal (mean and standard deviation)
  • Binomial (trials and probability)
  • Poisson (mean)

Normal Distribution

Example calculation of normal distribution

Population <- rnorm(100, 30, 3)
Sample <- sample(Population, 20)
par(mfrow=c(1,2))    
hist(Population, col = 'blue', border = 'white', xlab = "Population")
hist(Sample, col='red', border='white', xlab = 'Sample')

Binomial Distribution

Example calculation of binomial distribution

Population <- rbinom(100, 5, 0.5)
Sample <- sample(Population, 20)
par(mfrow=c(1,2))    
hist(Population, col = 'blue', border = 'white', xlab = "Population")
hist(Sample, col='red', border='white', xlab = 'Sample')
